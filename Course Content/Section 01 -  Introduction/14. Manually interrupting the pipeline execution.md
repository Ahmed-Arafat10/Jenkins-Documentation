### 14. Manually Interrupting the Pipeline Execution (2)

In Jenkins, most jobs execute very quickly—often in just a few seconds. However, sometimes jobs may misbehave by running much longer than expected or even getting stuck. In such cases, it becomes necessary to **manually interrupt the pipeline execution**.

---

#### ✅ Example of a Long-Running Job

* Normally, builds finish in **2–3 seconds**, with artifact creation in milliseconds.
* If we add a command like:

  ```groovy
  sleep 600
  ```

  This instructs Jenkins to pause for 600 seconds (10 minutes).
* While "sleep" is just simulating a long task, in real-world pipelines, you may have jobs stuck due to failed commands, deadlocks, or hanging processes.

---

#### ✅ Monitoring the Running Job

* Jenkins shows the **progress bar** during execution.
* By clicking the progress bar, you can view the **console output**.
* If the job is still active, you’ll see output indicators like `"...."` meaning Jenkins is waiting for completion.

---

#### ✅ Problem: Executor Blocking & Build Queue

* Jenkins agents (executors) have limited capacity (e.g., 2 parallel jobs).
* If a job hangs, it **blocks executors**, preventing other pipelines from running.
* Additional builds get pushed into the **build queue**, slowing down the entire system.

---

#### ✅ Solution: Manually Aborting the Job

1. Go to the running job.
2. Click the **“X” button** (visible in the job view or dashboard under *Build Executor Status*).
3. Jenkins will send an **interrupt signal** to the process.
4. The job status will change to **“Aborted”**, with a message like:

   ```
   Aborted by <username>
   ```
5. No artifacts are generated because the pipeline did not complete.

---

#### ✅ Key Takeaways

* Long-running or stuck jobs can block Jenkins and delay all other builds.
* Use the **abort feature** to free executors and clear the queue.
* Always monitor jobs that run unusually long compared to the baseline.
